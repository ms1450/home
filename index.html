ABOUT ME

"More than the sum of our experiences, our memories are the ultimate proof of reality." -Cogito ergo sum

Ever since 8th grade, when all my friends were playing soccer outside, I was in my school's the computer lab being fascinated at the possibilities. Coding, to me felt like a whole new reality where I could create anything I wanted, I realized then that coding has to be something that defines who I am. As I grew older, I became fascinated with all the different programming languages out there, starting from something as simple as Q-Basic, I began looking up all the different programming languages out there, reading tutorials and learning as much as I possibly could. With this, I was able to develop a strong skill set early on, and had a good understanding in most of the popular coding languages, this included Python, Java, C++ and even Scratch just for the sake of it.

As I became familiar with programming, I came across Ubuntu, as a kid who had only known about macOS and Windows, finding this whole new operating system changed my perspective on technology. I then began looking into different operating systems, their file structures and how to communicate with them. When I found out about Computer Security, I knew this was something I had to pursue. My Podcasts were now all related to CyberSecurity, I began practicing BASH coding and playing around Kali Linux in my free time. My favourite TV Show became Mr Robot.

Currently, I am a fourth-year student at Rochester Institute of Technology pursuing a bachelor's degree in Computing Security. I have participated in a ton of computing competitions and hackathons through which I learnt problem-solving and application of my programming skills to real world problems. One of my favourite competitions is the 'Red Vs Blue' competitions which involves two teams battling out in the digital warzone. The blue team, defending their infrastructure, attempting to protect and secure services, while the red team attacks it with custom tools and techniques, trying their best to maintain persistence. At the end, both teams coming together to discuss the actions they took and walking everyone through the process. This competition developed rapid response as well as familiarity with different systems. I have also interned at a Security Operation Center where I was able to hone my research and presentation skills as well as learn to use SIEM and SOAR. Some of my completed courses include Web Application Security, Cybersecurity Policy and Law, Cryptography, Database and Data Modelling, Cyber Defence Techniques and Network Services. I am also a member of my university's RITSEC club that discusses current security related events occurring throughout the world.
